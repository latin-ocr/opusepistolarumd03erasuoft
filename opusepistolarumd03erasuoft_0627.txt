the Church on the death of his wife. Papal favour created him Bp. of Feltre
1512 and Gardinal 1517. In 1518-9 he was in England as Legate to raise money
for the Crusade against the Turks (Ep. 887. 13 n.). On his departure Henry vm
made him English agent at the Papal Court, gave himsa house in Rome, and
promised him the bpric. of Salisbury at its next vacancy (Brewer iii. 119, 646).

1528—9 he was sent as Legate to England ou the question of the Divorce. See
DNB., Ciaconius iii. 384-6‚ and Gams. In Feb. 1520 Erasmus dedicated to
him his Paraphrase on Ephesians.

From the stress which in Epp. 968, 970, 990 Erasmus lays on Campegio's
interest in the English movement for the revival of learning, this letter may be
regarded as belonging to the group, Epp. 962, 964-7‚ in which an invitation to
return to England is evidently solicited. For the significance of the latter
